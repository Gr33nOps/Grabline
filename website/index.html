What it does

A real accelerator, a browser button, and yt-dlp — without the terminal.

Grabline pairs a proper download engine with the video tooling people usually fight a command line for.

Engine

Accelerated & crash-proof

Up to 128 connections per file with dynamic segmentation — free connections steal work from the slowest. HTTP/2, retry-forever with smart backoff, and checkpointed resume that survives a kill or a power cut.

Browser

One button, every page

Hover the ⬇ on any video, right-click anything, or grab every link or image on a page. It talks to the app over Native Messaging — no open ports.

Video & audio

Pick the quality you want

4K → 144p with size estimates, MP3/M4A/FLAC with tags and cover art, subtitles, clip trimming, SponsorBlock, chapters, and a cookies field for login-gated videos — on YouTube and 1000+ sites, powered by yt-dlp. Media on sites yt-dlp doesn't know still downloads via a best-effort page scrape.

Queue

A real queue manager

Unlimited named queues with sequential or parallel modes, per-queue schedules, category auto-assign, and dependencies (“download B after A”). Download later at a set time, weekday/weekend rules, battery mode, resume-on-reconnect, completion sounds & scripts, and sleep / hibernate / shutdown / lock when done.

Streams

HLS & DASH, reassembled

Live and on-demand .m3u8 / .mpd streams are stitched into a clean .mp4 by FFmpeg, with quality picking and automatic retry.

Archives

A real archive manager

ZIP/TAR/GZ/BZ2/XZ built in, RAR/7Z via 7-Zip. Preview contents, extract only the files you pick, auto-extract after download, remembered passwords, and an optional pre-extract virus scan (Defender/ClamAV).

Torrents

A real torrent client built in

Magnets & .torrent files, DHT, PEX, UPnP/NAT-PMP, web seeds, file selection, sequential streaming mode, seed-ratio limits, RSS auto-download, and torrent creation — powered by libtorrent, the engine behind qBittorrent.

Cloud

Cloud storage & file servers

SFTP, FTP/FTPS, SCP, S3 and WebDAV with resume and saved logins (kept in your system keychain), plus Google Drive / Dropbox / OneDrive / Nextcloud share links turned into direct downloads. Grab a whole remote folder at once.

Files

Your downloads, organized

Auto-sorted into Video / Music / Images / Documents / Archives / Programs / Games / Torrents. Rename rules, duplicate detection with hash-compare merging, favorite folders, and searchable tags & notes per download.

Extras

The small stuff, done

URL patterns like file[1-100].jpg, drag-and-drop, checksum verify, video → GIF, dark/light theme, start-in-tray.

First run

Installed in three steps. No config files, no terminal.

Download & run the installer

Grabline appears in your Start Menu / Spotlight / app grid like any other program — and pairs itself with your browsers on first launch.

Add the browser button

The setup window detects your default browser and offers a one-click Add to your browser. The extension ships inside the app.

Grab anything

Paste a URL, drop it on the window, or click the ⬇ in your browser. FFmpeg installs itself from Settings when you first need MP3 or streams.

Honest by design

What Grabline won’t do.

No DRM circumvention

Netflix, Prime Video, Disney+, Spotify tracks and friends are refused with a clear message, never a workaround.

No login bypass

The optional browser-session feature uses your login for your content — cookies stay in memory, never stored or sent.

No telemetry, ever

No analytics, no accounts, no phone-home — in any part of the project. Everything happens on your machine.
