The trick

Grab the line. Keep it busy.

One connection is easy for a server to throttle. GrabLine cuts the file into segments and pulls them together (up to 128 lines). When one segment crawls, an idle line steals its work. You see the speed live. If the power drops, progress is already on disk.

  • Many sockets, one file. Beginners just hit Download. Power users dial connections, proxies and caps.
  • Crash friendly. Restart GrabLine and unfinished jobs pick up where they stopped.
  • A dashboard that tells the truth. Current, average, peak, ETA, and which lines are earning their keep.
Video and audio

Choose the cut before it lands.

Hover a video in the browser, or paste a link. GrabLine shows resolutions, audio only (MP3, M4A, FLAC), subtitles, chapters, SponsorBlock, and a trim slider. One decision. Then it runs. A thousand plus sites via yt dlp.

  • New here? Pick Best or 1080p and go. The defaults are sane.
  • Know what you want? Exact height, codec, subs, clip in and out, then Download.

Still curious

Quick answers

Short takes on what the page above left open.

How is it different from IDM?

Same big idea: many connections and a browser button. GrabLine is open source, runs on macOS and Linux too, and folds video, audio, torrents, cloud and archives into one app. We do not phone home with your habits.

Which browsers get the GrabLine button?

Firefox has a signed add on. Chrome, Edge and Brave pair from Browser Setup inside the app. No extension? Paste the URL. Either way, hand off is a local pipe, not a network port.

Where does my stuff live?

Files land in the folder you pick. Settings and the job database sit in the normal app data directory on your computer. Session cookies for “use my browser” never leave your disk.

Do I pay anything?

No. AGPL 3.0, no paid tier, no trial clock. Source is on GitHub if you want to build it yourself.
